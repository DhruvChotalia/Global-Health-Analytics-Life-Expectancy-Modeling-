## Project Overview
An in-depth statistical analysis of global health indicators from 2000‚Äì2015. This project identifies the socio-economic and medical drivers behind human longevity, providing data-driven insights for global health policymakers and NGOs.

## Key Statistical Findings
* **Hypothesis Testing:** Conducted **Two-Tailed Z-Tests** ($Z = -2.057$, $p = 0.0397$) to evaluate global health benchmarks against current data.
* **Predictive Drivers:** Identified **Schooling ($r = 0.73$)** and **Immunization ($r = 0.64$)** as the most significant positive predictors of life expectancy.
* **Variance Analysis:** Used the **Coefficient of Determination ($R^2 = 0.89$)** to demonstrate that adult mortality rates explain the vast majority of lifespan variance.
* **Confidence Estimation:** Developed 75%, 85%, and 95% Confidence Intervals to provide quantifiable certainty for population mean estimates.
